Melanie Adams suggested the following:

  • Quinoa is a healthy cereal that you can buy in bulk. It is delicious in salads or as a side dish.
  • Frozen fruit is cheaper than fresh fruit. Add it to smoothies, yogurt or oatmeal, or in ready meals.
  • Chickpeas have a high protein content and are versatile. Very good in salads, they also make an excellent crispy snack when cooked in an air fryer.

"Mindful eating" applies the concept of mindfulness (paying full attention to one’s experiences) to food.
It invites us to think about what we eat and why we eat, as well as to appreciate our food.
To practice it, you have to eat when you are hungry and stop as soon as you feel full.
We must also learn to differentiate real hunger from the desire to eat emotions.

Labels tell us a lot about what is in what we eat.
They include a nutrition facts table and a list of ingredients.
These are always listed in order, starting with the one that is in larger quantity.

More and more studies are showing potential health benefits of organic foods:

  • More antioxidants.
  • More omega-3 fatty acids.
